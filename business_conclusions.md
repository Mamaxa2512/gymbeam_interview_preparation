# Business Conclusions

## 1. Price Change Summary

Out of 20 products, **12 require pricing action**, broken down as follows:

| Action                 | Count | Products                                      |
|------------------------|-------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Price reduced          | 8     | P001, P005, P006, P010, P011, P015, P016, P020 |
| Price at floor (15%)   | 4     | P002, P007, P012, P017                        |
| Competitor below cost  | 4     | P003, P008, P013, P018                        |
| No change needed       | 4     | P004, P009, P014, P019                        |

8 products can be directly reduced to the competitor's price while maintaining a healthy margin (≥15%). 4 additional products need a price reduction but are capped at the 15% margin floor to protect profitability.

## 2. Margin Impact

The repricing strategy reduces total expected margin (across all stock) from **€107,283 to €79,595** — a decrease of **€27,688 (−25.8%)**. While this is a significant reduction, it makes 12 out of 20 products competitively priced, which is likely to increase sales volume and conversion rates. If the price reduction drives even a moderate increase in unit sales for the affected products, the total margin impact could be neutral or positive. I would recommend implementing the changes in phases and monitoring conversion rates over 2–4 weeks before a full rollout.

## 3. Risk Products

Products **P003** (Google Pixel 8 Pro), **P008** (Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon), **P013** (Meta Quest 3 128GB), and **P018** (Keychron K2 V2 Keyboard) are at the highest risk. The competitor is already selling these items below our cost price, which may indicate dumping, stock liquidation, or a loss-leader strategy designed to attract customers. I would recommend investigating whether we can negotiate better terms with our current supplier or find an alternative one. If the competitor's pricing is sustained long-term, we should evaluate whether these products are still worth carrying in our assortment, or if we should shift focus to higher-margin alternatives within the same categories.

## 4. Category Recommendation

I would recommend a deeper analysis of the **Smartphones** category. Out of 5 smartphone products, 3 require pricing action (P001 — price reduced, P002 — price at floor, P003 — competitor below cost), making it the most impacted category proportionally. Additionally, smartphones tend to have higher absolute price points, so even small margin percentage changes translate into significant monetary impact. The category also contains the most diverse set of scenarios (A, B, and C), which suggests pricing pressure from the competitor is strongest here. Understanding whether this is a temporary competitive push or a structural advantage would be critical for our pricing strategy going forward.
